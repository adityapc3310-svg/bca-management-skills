Five Pillars of BCA Management

Each pillar combines theoretical frameworks with hands-on application — preparing you to lead technical teams, manage stakeholders, and drive strategic outcomes.

Translate complex technical concepts for diverse audiences. Master executive writing, stakeholder presentations, cross-cultural communication, and technical storytelling.

Project Management

Agile, Scrum, Kanban, Waterfall — end-to-end delivery mastery. Sprint planning, risk management, resource allocation, and industry tools (Jira, Trello, Asana, MS Project).

Course Structure

Semester-wise Curriculum

Progressive learning path from fundamentals to advanced applications — each semester builds on the previous.

Semester 1 — Foundations

Business Communication Fundamentals

Professional writing, presentation basics, email etiquette, meeting facilitation, and communication frameworks for technical professionals.

Professional WritingPresentation SkillsMeeting FacilitationCommunication Frameworks
Semester 1 — Foundations

Management Principles & Org Behavior Basics

Classical and contemporary management theories, organizational structures, motivation fundamentals, and introduction to organizational culture.

Management TheoriesOrg StructuresMotivation BasicsCulture Intro
Semester 2 — Core Skills

Project Management: Agile & Waterfall

Complete project lifecycle: initiation, planning, execution, monitoring, closure. Scrum Master practices, sprint ceremonies, backlog grooming, and scaling frameworks.

Agile/ScrumWaterfallRisk ManagementJira/MS Project
Semester 2 — Core Skills

Leadership Essentials & Strategic Thinking

Leadership styles, situational leadership, delegation frameworks, coaching, feedback models, and introduction to strategic planning methodologies.

Leadership StylesDelegationCoachingStrategy Intro
Semester 3 — Advanced Applications

Advanced Project Management & Change Management

Program management, portfolio management, organizational change models (ADKAR, Kotter), stakeholder mapping, and digital transformation leadership.

Program ManagementChange ModelsStakeholder MappingDigital Transformation
Semester 3 — Advanced Applications

Negotiation, Conflict Resolution & Decision Making

Principled negotiation (Harvard method), mediation techniques, cognitive biases in decision making, data-informed vs data-driven decisions, and ethics.

Harvard NegotiationMediationCognitive BiasesDecision Ethics
Semester 4 — Capstone & Career

Integrated Capstone Project

12-week industry consulting engagement. Define scope, manage delivery, present to stakeholders. Portfolio-ready deliverable with client testimonial.

Client EngagementScope DefinitionDelivery ManagementStakeholder Presentation
Semester 4 — Capstone & Career

Industry Internship & Portfolio Development

Structured internship with partner organizations. Career coaching, mock interviews, resume workshops, and professional portfolio curation.

InternshipCareer CoachingMock InterviewsPortfolio Review
